OVERVIEW:

Char Dham Trip:

the Do Dham Yatra is commonly known as the pilgrimage to Kedarnath and Badrinath temples, which hold immense religious significance in Hindu mythology. Badrinath is the sacred place of Lord Vishnu in the dual form of Nar Narayan, while Kedarnath is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva.

ITINERY:

Budget Trip plan to Char Dham:


  • Distance: Approximately 2,100 km (by air)
  • Fly from Chennai to Delhi. Upon arrival, check into your hotel and relax. Prepare for the journey to Uttarakhand.

  • Distance: Approximately 230 km (by road).
  • Drive or take a train from Delhi to Haridwar. This city, located on the banks of the Ganges River, is a major pilgrimage site. Visit the Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri in the evening.

  • Distance: Approximately 220 km (by road)
  • Drive from Haridwar to Barkot. Barkot is a picturesque town that serves as a base for the Yamunotri pilgrimage. Enjoy the serene landscapes and prepare for the trek to Yamunotri.

  • Distance: Approximately 40 km (by road) + 6 km (by trek)
  • Drive to Janki Chatti and then trek to Yamunotri. Yamunotri is the source of the Yamuna River and is famous for its thermal springs and the Yamunotri Temple. Return to Barkot for an overnight stay.

  • Distance: Approximately 90 km (by road).
  • Drive to Uttarkashi, which is situated on the banks of the Bhagirathi River. Visit the Kashi Vishwanath Temple and explore the local sights.

  • Distance: Approximately 100 km (by road).
  • Drive to Gangotri, the source of the Ganges River. Visit the Gangotri Temple and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains. Return to Uttarkashi for the night.

  • Distance: Approximately 250 km (by road).
  • Drive to Guptkashi. This town is the gateway to Kedarnath and offers beautiful views of the Himalayas. Visit the Vishwanath Temple in Guptkashi.

  • Distance: Approximately 30 km (by road) + 16 km (by trek).
  • Drive to Sonprayag and then take a shuttle to Gaurikund. From there, trek to Kedarnath. Kedarnath is one of the 12 Jyotirlingas and is renowned for its temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Stay overnight in Kedarnath

  • Distance: 16 km (by trek) + 30 km (by road).
  • Trek back from Kedarnath to Gaurikund and then drive to Guptkashi. Relax and recover from the trek.

  • Distance: Approximately 200 km (by road)
  • Drive to Rishikesh, a town known for its yoga and spiritual significance. Enjoy the peaceful environment and perhaps attend a yoga session or meditation class.

  • Distance: Approximately 250 km (by road)
  • Drive to Delhi. Spend the day exploring the city or shopping. Prepare for your return journey.

  • Distance: Approximately 2,100 km (by air).
  • Fly back to Chennai. Your Char Dham Yatra concludes here.
